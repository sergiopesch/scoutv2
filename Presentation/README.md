# Scout one-minute market vision

Open `index.html` in a modern browser. The five-slide deck is designed for a 60-second builder-focused delivery and does not require a build step or network access for its visuals. Source links require an internet connection when opened.

## One-minute pacing

- `00:00–00:18`: investment versus serious business results
- `00:18–00:30`: customer call → handoffs → “that’s not what I wanted”
- `00:30–00:42`: Scout finds what matters; Codex powers it
- `00:42–00:52`: conversation → right problem → approval → Codex build
- `00:52–01:00`: stop pitching pilots; start shipping value

## Presenter controls

- `→`, `Space`, or `Page Down`: reveal the next storytelling beat; after the final beat, advance to the next slide
- `←` or `Page Up`: hide the current beat; from the opening beat, return to the previous slide fully revealed
- `Home` / `End`: first / last slide
- `F`: enter or exit fullscreen
- `N`: toggle private speaker notes

The market figures on slide 1 are separately sourced signals and must not be presented as a single ROI calculation. McKinsey’s 6% refers specifically to surveyed organizations classified as AI high performers: at least 5% enterprise-level EBIT impact from AI plus reported significant AI value. Press `N` for the timecoded script and the intended caveats.

Motion is presenter-controlled so each idea appears on your cue: the opening market statement starts full-stage and compresses into its eyebrow position, the market numbers land, engagement handoffs cascade, Scout resolves into view, and the Codex build card powers up. Speaker notes mark every reveal with `→`. The deck respects the operating system’s reduced-motion preference.
